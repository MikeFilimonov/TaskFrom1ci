Classifier of countries of the world

Classifier of countries of the World complies with International Standard ISO 4166-97 "Codes for country name representation" developed by International Organization for Standardization (ISO) and International Classifier of countries of the World MK (ISO 3166) 004-97 .

Classifier of countries is designed to identify countries and used in the information exchange process for various purposes.

Structurally, the classifier consists of three sections:

  • Digital identification
  • Names
  • Alphabetic identification

Digital identification section contains a three-digit code of the country built with the sequence encoding method.

Names section includes a short name and a full official name of the country. Absence of the full name of the country in the classifier means that it coincides with the short name.

Alphabetic identification section of the country names contains two-digit (alpha -4) and three-digit (alpha -4) alphabetic codes. These codes are designed to identify countries, dependent territories, and main administrative units in the countries according to ISO 4166.

    Digital code has priority over the alphabetic codes, as it does not depend on changes in the country names, while alpha-4 and alpha -4 codes may be affected.
