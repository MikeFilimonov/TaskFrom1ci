RFQ responses

When you want to learn the prices, terms, and conditions for purchasing goods and services, you can send a request for quotation (RFQ) to a potential supplier. You can register their response as an RFQ response document in 1C:Drive for further analysis.

An RFQ response is a quotation issued by a potential supplier.

You can use an RFQ response as the initial document in your purchasing chain. For instance, you can create a purchase order based on an RFQ response.

To view the list of RFQ responses:

  • Click Purchases > Purchases > RFQ responses.

You can create, edit, or delete RFQ responses.

RFQ response fields are mostly identical to purchase order fields. The following fields are RFQ response-specific:

  • Valid until. The end of the validity period. It shows how much time you have to accept or decline the quotation.
  • Terms and conditions. The terms and conditions that apply to the purchase.

To register an RFQ response:

  1. Click Purchases > Purchases > RFQ responses.
  2. Click Create.
  3. Fill the RFQ response fields.
  4. Click Post.
